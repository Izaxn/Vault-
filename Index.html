The Story

How the Vault Started

Noor started doing nails at her own kitchen table — for herself first, then for whoever asked next. What stood out early was how much of a typical salon visit is designed around getting people back out the door: a rushed fill, minimal conversation, a set that looks fine under the salon lights and rougher two weeks later.

Noorvaultnails is built the opposite way. One client in the studio at a time, so there's no dividing attention between three sets at once. Tools are sterilized between every single appointment, not batch-cleaned at closing. Full sets are shaped to hold up to actual hands — dishes, gym bags, keyboards — not just to photograph well the day they're done.

The "vault" part isn't just branding. It's the idea that a set worth paying for is something you hold onto: you remember what you were doing the week you wore it, you don't think twice before someone asks to see your hands, and you're not counting days until the next appointment out of dread. That's the standard every session gets built around.

  • One client, one artist, one set at a time
  • Tools sterilized appointment-to-appointment
  • Sessions built around your actual hands, not a template
  • Space to ask questions if this is new to you

— Noor

The Craft

What "Custom" Actually Means Here

Charms and designs aren't stuck on top of a finished set at the end — they're built into how the set is shaped from the start.

✦

Charms set in, not glued on

Charms are placed into wet acrylic or gel and sealed under a top layer, not glued to the surface afterward. That's the difference between a charm that survives three weeks of normal hands and one that catches on your sleeve by day four.

✦

Sculpted shape, not a press-on topper

Long, coffin, or XL sets are sculpted directly onto your natural nail with acrylic, then shaped by hand — not built as a pre-formed tip with a topper glued over it. It takes longer, and it's why the shape still looks intentional at the two-week mark.

✦

Freehand line work

Fine detail and line art are done freehand with a brush, so no two "matching" sets actually match pixel for pixel — small variation is part of it being made by hand instead of stamped.

✦

Shape fit to how you actually use your hands

If you type all day or work with your hands, that changes what length and curve will actually hold up. Part of the consultation at the start of your appointment is talking through that before any acrylic goes on.

Between Visits

Keeping Your Set Vault-Worthy

A set is only as good as how it's treated between appointments. A few habits that make the difference.

Daily

  • Cuticle oil once a day keeps the nail bed flexible and slows lifting
  • Gloves for dishes, cleaning, and anything with harsh chemicals
  • Use the pad of your finger, not the nail, as a tool — no popping tabs or peeling stickers

Know the Difference

  • Normal wear: slight growth gap near the cuticle after 2 weeks
  • Come back sooner: a full nail lifting, a crack across the acrylic, or a charm coming loose
  • Catching either early keeps the set — and your natural nail underneath — in better shape
